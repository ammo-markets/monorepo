## Ammunition Terms

**Caliber**
The internal diameter of a firearm barrel, used to identify ammunition size. Examples: 9mm, 5.56 NATO, .308 Winchester.

**FMJ (Full Metal Jacket)**
A bullet type with a soft lead core enclosed in a harder metal shell. Used primarily for target practice and range shooting. Less expensive than hollow points.

**JHP (Jacketed Hollow Point)**
A bullet designed to expand on impact. Used for self-defense. The hollow cavity in the tip causes the bullet to mushroom, transferring more energy to the target.

**Brass Case**
Ammunition casing made from brass. Standard for quality practice and defensive ammunition. Can be reloaded (reused).

**Grain (gr)**
The unit of measurement for bullet weight. One grain equals 1/7000 of a pound. Example: a 115gr 9mm bullet weighs 115 grains.

**Round**
A single complete unit of ammunition — the cartridge case, primer, propellant, and bullet together. "One round" = one shot.

## Protocol Terms

**Primers**
The Ammo Exchange liquidity incentive points program. Earn Primers by participating in the protocol. (Details to be announced.)

**Mint**
The act of depositing stablecoins to receive ammunition tokens. The smart contract creates new tokens in exchange for your deposit.

**Redeem**
The act of burning ammunition tokens to receive physical ammunition. Tokens are locked in the contract during fulfillment and burned upon completion.

**Oracle**
The on-chain price feed that provides current ammunition prices. Updated every 4 hours from AmmoSquared data.

**Self-Rescue**
The ability to cancel a pending redemption and recover your tokens if the fulfillment deadline expires. Built into the smart contract — no trust required.

## Crypto / DeFi Terms

**DEX (Decentralized Exchange)**
A peer-to-peer marketplace for trading tokens without a central intermediary. Examples: Trader Joe, Pangolin.

**ERC-20**
The standard interface for fungible tokens on EVM-compatible blockchains. Ammunition tokens are ERC-20s, making them compatible with wallets, DEXs, and DeFi protocols.

**Stablecoin**
A cryptocurrency pegged 1:1 to the US dollar. Ammo Exchange currently accepts **USDT** (Tether) for minting, with **USDC** (Circle) support planned.

**Wallet**
Software that stores your private keys and lets you interact with blockchain applications. Examples: MetaMask, Rabby, Coinbase Wallet.

**Gas**
The fee paid to the network for processing transactions. Gas is paid in the network's native token and is typically very low (under $0.10).

**SIWE (Sign-In with Ethereum)**
An open standard for authenticating to websites using your crypto wallet instead of a username and password. In Ammo Exchange, SIWE is only required during the redemption flow — to provide shipping information and verify your identity. All other actions (minting, viewing balances, market data) work with just a wallet connection.
